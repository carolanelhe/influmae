Who is Carol Anelhe

Sensory architect, atypical mom, and Harvard early intervention specialist

Carol grew up in Rio de Janeiro, started working at 16, and never stopped. Her path took her through a COVID recovery — during which she couldn't walk for months — all the way to Harvard, where she studied Early Childhood Development and early intervention in autism.

When her son Noah was born premature and later diagnosed with sensory processing disorder and autism, Carol did what she always does: she went all in. She studied, applied everything inside their home, and saw results in 4 months that no one expected.

Today she turns the knowledge she gained at Harvard — and the hard-won experience of being an atypical mom — into practical tools so no other mom has to figure this out alone.
